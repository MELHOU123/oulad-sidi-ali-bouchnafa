Genealogical tradition

According to Arabic genealogical literature, the Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa are presented as a sharifian lineage linked to the Idrisids. In this tradition, Sidi Ali Bouchnafa is considered a descendant of the Idrisid house through Sidi Ahmad, a descendant of Idris II, also known as Idris al-Azhar, and of Idris I, founder of the Idrisid dynasty in Morocco.

One genealogical chain attributed in Arabic genealogical sources to Ahmad al-Shabbani al-Idrisi presents Sidi Ali Bouchnafa as:

سيدي علي بوشنافة بن علي بن عمرو بن خالد بن زكرياء بن محمد بن زكرياء بن محمد بن يحيى بن زكرياء بن الحسن بن منصور بن جعفر بن محمد بن عبد الله بن أحمد بن محمد بن أبي العباس سيدي أحمد بن المولى إدريس الأزهر.

Methodological note: This lineage is presented as genealogical tradition. It should not be treated as an undisputed historical fact unless supported by additional independent historical research.

Key terms

Idrisid Relating to the Idrisid dynasty and its descendants
Sharifian A lineage claiming descent from the Prophet Muhammad through Hasan or Husayn
Tradition Attributed genealogical memory, not necessarily documentary proof
