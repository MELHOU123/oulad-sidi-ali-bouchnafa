Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa

A neutral English-language information page about the tribe of Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa, its location, tribal branches, local memory, and genealogical tradition.

Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa, also transcribed as Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchenafa, Ouled Sidi Ali Bouchnafa, Oulad Sidi Ali Bou Chnafa, or in Arabic أولاد سيدي علي بوشنافة, is a tribe of eastern Morocco, historically associated with the high plains of the Oriental region, including the areas of Berguent, Jerada, Aïn Bni Mathar and Tendrara.

The tribe is documented in academic and regional sources about eastern Morocco. The geographer Roland Paskoff mentioned the Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchenafa in his 1957 study of the high plains of eastern Morocco and the Berguent region. Regional documents from the Agence de l'Oriental also mention Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa among the tribal groups connected with the early workforce of the mining town of Jerada.

Note: The genealogical material on this page is presented as attributed tradition, not as an undisputed historical fact. Dates of birth and death for Sidi Ali Bouchnafa have not been confirmed in reliable published sources.

Location

The tribe is located in eastern Morocco, within the present-day Oriental region. Sources associate it with the areas of Jerada, Aïn Bni Mathar, Mrija, Oulad Ghzil, Berguent and Tendrara.

Roland Paskoff mentioned the Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchenafa in his article Les Hautes Plaines du Maroc oriental : la région de Berguent, published in Cahiers d'outre-mer in 1957.

History

The history of the tribe is linked to the high plains of eastern Morocco and to the pastoral spaces situated between Jerada, Berguent, Aïn Bni Mathar and Tendrara.

According to an article published by Al Molahid Journal about a cultural event held in the commune of Oulad Ghzil, in Jerada Province, local tribal memory identifies Sidi Ali Bouchnafa as the eponymous ancestor of the tribe. The same source states that his tomb is located in the area of Jbel Aksan, near Wadi Essouan, in the surroundings of Tendrara, Figuig Province.

The same source also states that the tribe remained for a long period in the Abreida area before moving to other places, and that at the beginning of the 16th century it settled in the Mrija area, where it found more favorable conditions for settlement.

In the modern history of Jerada, regional documents on the heritage of the Oriental region mention the Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa among the tribes of the area that contributed to the early workforce of the mining town of Jerada.

Sidi Ali Bouchnafa

According to the local tradition reported by Al Molahid Journal, Sidi Ali Bouchnafa is identified as Sidi Ali ben Abd Allah, nicknamed “Bouchnafa”. The same source places his tomb at Jbel Aksan, near Wadi Essouan, in the surroundings of Tendrara, Figuig Province.

Other genealogical traditions cited in Arabic genealogical works and local accounts mention Abreida, Jbel Aksan and Wadi al-Awan or Wadi Essouan as places associated with his burial, and refer to Manada as a place connected with his family origin. These details should be treated as genealogical tradition rather than confirmed historical data.

No reliable sources have been located that provide precise dates of birth or death for Sidi Ali Bouchnafa.

Tribal organization

According to the local tradition reported by Al Molahid Journal, the tribe of Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa is traditionally organized into three main branches: Oulad Ghzil, Aouamer and Oulad Bouras.

Oulad Ghzil
أولاد اغزيل

The branch of Oulad Ghzil is centered on Hssiane Ed-Diab, the center of the present territorial commune of Oulad Ghzil. Its douars or local fractions include:

  • الحومر
  • الجبابرة
  • أولاد سعيد بلحاج
  • أولاد قدور
  • أولاد مسعود
  • لݣطيات
  • أولاد موسى
  • أولاد يوسف
  • أولاد بنيعݣوب

Aouamer
لعوامر

The branch of Aouamer is centered on Mrija. According to the cited source, it is composed of five fractions:

  • الحميمات
  • الشلافة
  • لخرانݣة
  • أولاد دادو
  • الدوينات

Oulad Bouras
أولاد بوراس

The branch of Oulad Bouras is also centered on Mrija. According to the cited source, it is composed of eight fractions:

  • أولاد لغريب
  • النݣاݣزة
  • الشريطات
  • لعوافة

Genealogical tradition

According to Arabic genealogical literature, the Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa are presented as a sharifian lineage linked to the Idrisids. In this tradition, Sidi Ali Bouchnafa is considered a descendant of the Idrisid house through Sidi Ahmad, a descendant of Idris II, also known as Idris al-Azhar, and of Idris I, founder of the Idrisid dynasty in Morocco.

One genealogical chain attributed in Arabic genealogical sources to Ahmad al-Shabbani al-Idrisi presents Sidi Ali Bouchnafa as:

سيدي علي بوشنافة بن علي بن عمرو بن خالد بن زكرياء بن محمد بن زكرياء بن محمد بن يحيى بن زكرياء بن الحسن بن منصور بن جعفر بن محمد بن عبد الله بن أحمد بن محمد بن أبي العباس سيدي أحمد بن المولى إدريس الأزهر.

According to this tradition, the tribe preserves the memory of its Idrisid origin through Sidi Ali Bouchnafa. However, this lineage should be presented as attributed genealogical tradition, not as an undisputed historical fact.

Cultural heritage

The tribe preserves elements of intangible heritage connected with tribal memory, community celebrations, folklore, traditional music, horsemanship and the gathering known locally as Daïrat al-Maârouf or Moussem al-Dayer. According to Al Molahid Journal, this celebration is held in the commune of Oulad Ghzil and lasts several days, with the participation of inhabitants of the Oriental region.
