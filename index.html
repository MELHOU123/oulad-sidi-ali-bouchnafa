Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa, also transcribed as Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchenafa, Ouled Sidi Ali Bouchnafa, Oulad Sidi Ali Bou Chnafa, or in Arabic أولاد سيدي علي بوشنافة, is a tribe of eastern Morocco, historically associated with the high plains of the Oriental region, including Berguent, Jerada, Aïn Bni Mathar and Tendrara.

The tribe is documented in academic and regional sources about eastern Morocco. Roland Paskoff mentioned the Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchenafa in his 1957 study of the high plains of eastern Morocco and the Berguent region. Regional documents from the Agence de l'Oriental also mention Oulad Sidi Ali Bouchnafa among the tribal groups connected with the early workforce of the mining town of Jerada.

Important note: The genealogical material is presented as attributed Arabic genealogical tradition, not as an undisputed historical fact. No reliable published source has been found giving precise dates of birth or death for Sidi Ali Bouchnafa.

What this website covers

Location

Eastern Morocco, especially areas associated with Jerada, Aïn Bni Mathar, Berguent, Mrija, Oulad Ghzil and Tendrara.

Branches

Traditional branches include Oulad Ghzil, Aouamer and Oulad Bouras, with local fractions listed in Arabic sources.

Genealogy

The tribe is linked in genealogical tradition to Sidi Ali Bouchnafa and an Idrisid lineage, with attribution to Arabic genealogical sources.
